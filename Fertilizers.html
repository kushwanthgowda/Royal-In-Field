FERTILIZERS



Fertilizers provide plant nutrients, commercially manufactured using chemicals. Fertilizers supply Nitrogen, Prosperous and Potassium (NPK). They are used to good vegetative growth, giving a rise to healthy plants. Fertilizers are one of the major components for obtaining higher yields especially in expensive farming practices. Fertilizers contain much higher amount of nutrients in comparison to the manures and are, therefore, used in very small quantities. A complete fertilizer is one which contains all the three critical elements or minerals, nitrogen, prosperous and potassium. These fertilizers may supply one or more nutrients. Chemically they may be inorganic compounds or organic compounds. On the basis of availability of nutrients from them, fertilizers are divided into following four groups:
  1. Nitrogenous Fertilizers
  2. Phosphatic Fertilizers
  3. Potassic Fertilizers
  4. Complex Fertilizers

BIOFERTILIZERS

Organisms which enrich the soil with nutrients are called Biofertilizer. Biofertilizers are used for the specific crop plants such as pulses, legumes, oil seeds and rice. Biofertilizers are renewable and non pollutant sources of plant nutrients such as nitrogen. They are not alternatives to chemical fertilizers but can play a supplementary role is supplying nitrogen to specific crops under specific soil conditions. Nitrogen fixing microorganisms, i.e., non-symbolitic and symbolitic cyanobacteria and phosphate-solubilising microorganisms are the main type of biofertilizer that are being used in India.
